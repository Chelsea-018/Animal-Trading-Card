Rex rabbit

The term rex rabbit (without capitalization) refers informally to one of at least eight breeds of domestic rabbit.

Scientific Name: Oryctolagus cuniculus

Average length: 3.4-4.8 kg (7.5-10.5 lb)

Average lifespan: 6-8yrs

Habitat: Low grasslands and forested areas.

Praedators: Humans, wild/domestic dogs and cats, and birds of prey.

Diet: Hay, fresh vegetables, and a small amount of high-quality pellet food.

The breed originated in France in 1919. Its origin was a litter of wild gray rabbits and has been developed over the years by fanciers and the fur industry. The Rex Rabbit was first shown publicly at the Paris International Rabbit Show in 1924 and has been recognized as a standard breed in parts of Europe since 1925.
